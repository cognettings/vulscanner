Changes you should expect this month on our platform

1

Smooth root movement between groups:

We want to ensure that when you move a Git root from one group to another, the events and environment URLs associated with this root will also be transferred without any problem. The events will keep in their new group the status they had in the old one. And, in the latter, there will be a record that such events were moved.

2

Approval/rejection of temporary acceptance of vulnerabilities:

As is currently the case for requests of permanent acceptance of vulnerabilities, you will soon have the possibility to approve or reject requests of temporary acceptance of vulnerabilities and keep track of them from your organization's Policies section.

3

Add exclusions from the code:

We are working on implementing directives that your team can use to specify and provide a justification for which lines of the source code they do not want to be assessed by Machine. In other words, this would keep our tool from warning of vulnerabilities among those lines at your own risk.

4

New methods for Machine:

Our automated tool will identify new types of vulnerabilities thanks to these new methods:

  • CFN_IAM_EXCESSIVE_ROLE_POLICY

  • CONAN_CONANFILE_PY

  • CONAN_CONANFILE_PY_DEV

  • TFM_API_ALL_HTTP_METHODS_ENABLED

  • TFM_HTTP_METHODS_ENABLED

For more details, stay tuned for announcements on our News channel.
